Poems

Still Pine

Whispers echo in the forest’s chest,
Where time drips slow and roots find rest.
A needle falls—so gently, so sound—
Soft as silence on forest ground.

Blue Haze

Under twilight’s ocean sky,
I watch the mountains exhale a sigh.
The breeze knows things it’ll never say—
I breathe it in anyway.

Riverbed

Water hums beneath the stone,
A song that sings of being alone.
But not lonely, not today—
Just drifting gently on its way.

Cloud Mirror

The lake holds sky in open palms,
Reflecting both the rush and calm.
I sit, I watch, I start to feel
That maybe quiet things are real.

Rooted

I don’t need answers all the time,
Just a tree, a breeze, and space to climb.
Let the questions come and go—
Like leaves that fall, like streams that flow.
